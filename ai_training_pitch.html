The Opportunity

Most PMMs use AI like a search engine.
We can turn it into a system.

Right now, every PMM prompts from scratch, gets inconsistent output, and spends time reformatting to match BILL's voice. A shared system fixes that for all 20 of us at once.

1

Inconsistent starting points

Everyone has their own templates and prompts. When someone new joins or picks up a launch mid-stream, they're starting from zero. AI output quality depends entirely on who's prompting.

2

Brand voice is hard to scale

Our tone and voice guide exists, but every PMM interprets it slightly differently when writing or prompting. AI makes this worse unless we give it the same guardrails we follow ourselves.

3

Competitive intel goes stale

Market positioning changes fast. Manually tracking competitors across product pages, press releases, and review sites takes hours that most PMMs don't have in a launch cycle.

How It Works

We teach AI how BILL works, once. Then everyone benefits.

The system is only as good as what you feed it. By standardizing our inputs, we get consistent, on-brand output across the entire team without anyone needing to become a prompt engineer.

1 Shared Inputs

BILL Tone & Voice Guide

Brand personality, do's and don'ts, audience-specific language guidelines

PMM Instructions File

Behavioral rules for AI: no em dashes, no AI cliches, how to format output, what to avoid

Rollout Plan

Six weeks to get the full team running.

The rollout is designed so PMMs start producing real work in their first session. No theory-only workshops. Every session ends with a usable output.

Week 1

Kickoff: See What's Possible

Live walkthrough of the Transaction Agent launch from PRD to finished deliverables. Show the before and after. This is the credibility moment that gets buy-in from the room.

Live demo Full team (20 PMMs) 90 minutes
Week 2

Foundation: Same Playbook, Same Tools

Hands-on session where every PMM sets up their AI environment with the shared inputs: tone guide, templates, instructions file, and GTM deliverables matrix. Everyone leaves with AI configured to sound like BILL.

Hands-on workshop Setup & configuration 2 hours
Weeks 3-4

Build: Real Workflows on Real Projects

PMMs pair up by experience level and build a reusable workflow for their product area. Each pair takes a current launch or project and runs it through the system. Office hours format with hands-on support.

Open Questions

Things we need to figure out together.

This proposal is a starting point for the conversation, not a finished plan. These are the questions we need to answer before moving forward.

Access

How does IT feel about this? How do we get access to Claude?

Claude is the tool that powers these workflows. We need to understand IT's stance on approving it for the team, what security reviews are needed, and what the procurement process looks like.

Tooling

Gemini is not Claude. They don't do the same things.

The workflows demonstrated here are built on Claude's specific capabilities, including coding, file access, web research, and iterative conversation. Gemini serves a different purpose and doesn't replicate what's shown in this proposal.

Access

There's a rumor that access may be opening back up.

If BILL is reconsidering broader AI tool access, this proposal could serve as a strong use case for why and how to do it well. A trained team with standardized guardrails is a better bet than individual experimentation.

Process

Do we want a more formal training program?

The six-week rollout is one approach. We could go lighter with self-serve resources and office hours, or more structured with a certification-style program. What level of investment makes sense for the team right now?

Tooling

How do we keep source documents live so AI stays current?

Templates, tone guides, and competitive data will evolve. We need a system where updates to these documents are automatically reflected in everyone's AI environment, so the team doesn't drift over time.

Adoption

How do we bring everyone along with us?

20 PMMs with different comfort levels, different product areas, and different workflows. Some will be excited, some will be skeptical. We need a plan that meets people where they are and builds confidence through early wins.

Let's build the most AI-ready PMM team in fintech.

The system works. The proof exists. The only question is how fast we want to scale it across the team.
